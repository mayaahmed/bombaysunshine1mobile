Chapter 5

Domestic Violence

Jaya's sister was getting married and Jaya was talking about the wedding all the time. Tara missed Manda very badly and decided to drop in at her home on her way back from college. The front door was closed. Tara heard some voices. She knew Manda usually left the back door open. So wanting to surprise Manda she entered through the back door.

Tara was shocked when she entered the house. Ajit was kicking Manda on the face with boots. Manda's face was bleeding. Manda's eight month's old baby was on her lap. Manda was protecting the child from Ajit's kicks.

They did not see Tara enter the room. May be if Tara was not so young she would have behaved differently. After wards Tara had wondered whether her mother would have behaved similarly. At that moment all that Tara could think was Ajit the enemy was hurting her sister. Tara took the first handy thing she found, a chair, and directed a full blow at Ajit which sent him reeling on the floor.

"Tara, stop. Don't. Don't" Manda screamed as Tara was going to hit Ajit again.

But Tara stayed poised thus for she thought that Ajit might attack her. After all she had hit him real hard. But to her surprise Ajit looked embarrassed. He got up slowly with a sheepish smile as though everything was a joke. And he stood there with the same stupid smile.

Tara realized that there was no physical danger from Ajit to her. Ajit, to Tara's reasoning, was the kind of man who could only hit somebody who would not hurt him back. Then Tara saw that Manda's face was bleeding badly. Tara went immediately to the fridge and got some ice cubes and started dressing Manda's wounds. This act of Tara broke Manda down completely. Manda started crying. Manda had accepted blows from her husband and harsh words from him as a part of being married. Manda never complained and she had gotten used to her life the way it was. But Tara's act of concern reminded her of the past when people were sensitive to her every whim and fancy. Manda realized that the life she lived now shocked the people who loved her. Manda wanted to say a lot of things but words failed her and she simply cried.

Ajit tried to come close to Manda. The baby was also crying. Tara went to the phone and called her mother. Right from the earlier days it was quite clear that Ajit was not exactly an ideal husband to Manda. He was short tempered and complained a lot about Manda. But Tara's parents had chosen to keep mum. Tara would defend her sister and yell at Ajit but it was all taken lightly. But now matters were serious because there was physical violence. Tara's parents were in a position from which they could not get out without doing something.

Tara realized that Manda had wanted to avoid putting her parents in that spot. Manda was ready to suffer in silence. And it wasn't as though Ajit was consistently bad. He was more like Jekyl and Hyde. He had two personalities. He was very loving at times and vicious at other times. Manda's parents came over and they decided to take Manda with them. Ajit wasn't ready to send Manda over and he was in tears. He said he was sorry and he did not know what came over him. Tara was surprised to see that Manda after a few hours of crying was quite willing to forgive and forget. And late at night Tara and her parents went back home without Manda.

But Tara could never forget what she had seen that evening. A lot of questions remained unanswered for her. She wondered whether Ajit hit her often. She looked for signs of violence every time Manda visited them. Tara reasoned that if Ajit really loved Manda as the others in her family were claiming then there could be no torture. Being loving at times was easy. Being kind consistently was the only relation based on mutual respect. Why did Manda not reason thus? Was Manda blindly in love with Ajit that it affected her reasoning? Or was it that any kind of relationship with a man was better than none? It was quite certain that it would have been difficult for Manda to get married again. That would mean no more relationship with a man because her society banned any relationship outside marriage. Or was it that Manda was afraid of society? Divorced women were not well received in society. Or was it that Manda was really happy married to Ajit? That was the most mind boggling question to Tara. Because all signs lead to this question.

Manda as always never had any complaints. She looked like a happily married woman every time Tara saw her.

"I am never going to be married" Tara declared to Jaya.

"Don't be an ass Tara" said Jaya. "Ajit obviously does not love your sister. That is why he hits her. Not all husbands do things like that. I am sure your husband is going to be henpecked."

Tara sighed and wondered what her husband would be like.
